Smaller files, same photo

A photo that's too large is a small but real annoyance. It won't attach to an email, it slows a website down, or it simply eats space you'd rather use for something else. Compressing fixes that by storing the same picture more efficiently — fewer bytes, same image. Done well, you genuinely can't see the difference.

You stay in control of how much to compress, and you see exactly what you saved after every file. There's no limit on how many images you can run, no account to create, and nothing is uploaded — the work happens right on your device.

How much smaller can you go?

It depends on the photo, but the savings are often dramatic. A typical phone photo of several megabytes can drop to a few hundred kilobytes while still looking sharp on screen. At a quality setting around 80%, most photos shrink by 70–90% with no visible loss. Images full of fine detail compress a little less; flat areas like skies compress a lot. The live savings figure takes the guesswork out of it.

Why does my PNG barely get smaller?

PNG is a lossless format, so there's little to squeeze out — especially for photographs, which it was never designed for. If you need a much smaller file and don't need a transparent background, convert the image to JPG or WebP instead. You can do that with the Convert image tool.

Frequently asked questions

Free, unlimited, and your photos never leave your device.

Is there a limit on how many images I can compress?

No. Compress as many as you want, as often as you want — no daily limit, no account, no waiting. Unlimited is the default here.

Will I lose quality?

Compression is a trade-off you control. At around 80–85% quality, the file gets much smaller with no loss the eye can see. Lower the slider for an even smaller file when screen display is all you need.

What quality setting should I use?

For most photos, 80–85% is the sweet spot — a big size drop with no visible loss. Go higher (90–95%) if the image is important or will be printed; go lower (70–80%) for web use where speed matters most.

Does compressing change the format?

No. A JPG stays a JPG and a PNG stays a PNG — the tool keeps your format and simply stores it more efficiently. To change format as well, use the Convert image tool.

Why is my PNG barely shrinking?

PNG is already lossless, so photos leave little room to compress. Converting to JPG or WebP will save far more space if you don't need transparency.

Are my photos uploaded anywhere?

No. Everything happens in your browser, on your device. Your images are never sent to a server — ideal for personal or sensitive photos.

Can I compress several photos at once?

Yes. Drop in a whole batch and each is compressed separately, then downloaded. There's no limit on how many.

Does it work on my phone?

Yes — any modern browser on iPhone, Android, tablet or computer. The work is done locally, so no fast connection is needed once the page loads.
