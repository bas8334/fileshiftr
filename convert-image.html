Not sure which to pick?

Sending a photo to someone or printing it? Choose JPG. Need a transparent background for a logo? Choose PNG. Putting images on a website you control? WebP gives the same quality at a smaller size. And if you're starting from an iPhone HEIC file, converting to JPG makes it open anywhere.

Why iPhone photos won't open on Windows

Since iOS 11, iPhones save photos as HEIC by default. It's a clever format — roughly half the size of a JPG at the same quality — but it's built on the HEVC video codec, which Windows, Android and most browsers don't read out of the box. That's why a photo that looks fine on your phone arrives as an unopenable file on someone else's computer. Converting HEIC to JPG solves it instantly: the photo becomes universally readable, at the small cost of a slightly larger file. You can convert as many as you need here, and because it happens on your device, your photos never leave it.

One thing worth knowing

Converting a JPG to PNG won't bring back detail that JPG compression already removed — once data is gone, no format can restore it. PNG simply stops any further loss. So if you have an original in a lossless format, convert from that whenever you can.

Can I convert iPhone photos (HEIC)?

Yes. Just drop your HEIC file — it's detected and converted automatically. The first time, your browser loads a small helper (~400 KB) and caches it; after that it's instant. Your files stay on your device throughout.

What's the difference between JPG and PNG?

JPG is built for photos: small files, but no transparency. PNG is built for graphics like logos and screenshots: it keeps crisp edges and transparent backgrounds, but files are larger. Rule of thumb: JPG for photographs, PNG for anything with text, lines, or transparency.

Should I use WebP?

If the image is going on a website you control, yes — WebP is typically 25–35% smaller than JPG at the same quality and is supported by every modern browser. For emailing or sharing with people, JPG is the safer universal choice.

Will converting reduce my image quality?

Converting between lossless formats (like PNG to WebP) keeps full quality. Converting to JPG applies compression, so you can trade a little quality for a smaller file — at 80–90% quality the difference is invisible for most photos.
