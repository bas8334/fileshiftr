About this project

Fileshiftr

A good tool does its job and then leaves you alone. That's exactly what Fileshiftr tries to be.

Why this?

The web has plenty of converters. Most of them are something other than they appear: the conversion is free, but your data, your attention or ultimately your money is the real product. That doesn't have to be bad — but it always means there's a wall somewhere.

Fileshiftr was built around a simpler idea: a converter is a tool. Like scissors, like a calculator. You pick it up when you need it, it does what it needs to do, you put it down.

No funnel, no waiting email list, no dashboard trying to pull you back. When your job is done, you're done with Fileshiftr — until next time.

How it works technically

Most file converters are web apps that send your files to a server, process them there, and send the result back. Fileshiftr does it differently: everything happens in your own browser.

Your files never leave your device — not temporarily stored, not encrypted and forwarded, not logged anywhere. This is not a promise but a technical reality.

Images are converted using your browser's built-in Canvas API. PDFs are processed with pdf-lib, a library that runs entirely client-side. For HEIC (iPhone photos) we load the heic2any library once; your browser caches it after that.

A nice side effect: you don't need a fast internet connection for the actual processing. After the page loads, Fileshiftr can work offline.

Honest about money

Free isn't the same as costless. Fileshiftr takes time and hosting, and is made and maintained by one person alongside other work. This isn't a startup pitch — just the reality of an independent project.
