Combine PDFs into one clean file

Sometimes a single document arrives in pieces — a contract split across separate scans, a report and its appendix, a stack of receipts you need to send together. Merging them into one PDF makes the whole thing easier to share, print and keep track of. Instead of attaching five files, you attach one.

Drop your PDFs in, arrange them in the order you want, and download the combined file. The pages are joined exactly as they are — no re-compression, no quality loss. And there's no limit on how many you can merge or how often, with no account and nothing uploaded to a server.

Does merging reduce quality?

No. A proper merge copies each page across exactly as it is, with no re-encoding. Text stays sharp and searchable, images keep their resolution, and embedded fonts and layout are preserved. The combined file looks identical to the originals — just in one document.

Good to know

Page order follows the order of your files, so arrange them before merging. If a PDF is password-protected, unlock it first. And because merging happens in your browser's memory, ten or twenty everyday PDFs combine smoothly — only very large batches will tax an older device.

Frequently asked questions

Free, unlimited, and your PDFs never leave your device.

Is there a limit on how many PDFs I can merge?

No. Merge as many files as you want, as often as you want — no cap, no account, no waiting. Unlimited is the default.

Are my PDFs uploaded to a server?

No. The entire merge happens in your own browser. Confidential documents — contracts, IDs, statements — never touch a server, because there's no server to touch.

Can I rearrange the pages before merging?

Yes. Once your files are added, drag them into any order you like. The final PDF follows that exact sequence.

Is the quality of my PDFs affected?

No. Pages are copied as-is with no re-encoding, so text stays selectable, images stay sharp, and the result looks identical to the originals.

Is there a maximum file size?

We set no fixed limit, but very large files use more of your browser's memory. Most modern browsers comfortably merge PDFs totalling around 100 MB.

Can I merge a password-protected PDF?

You'll need to unlock it first. Once a PDF can be opened without a password, it can be merged like any other.

Does it cost money?

No, it's free and always will be. If it saved you time, a coffee on Ko-fi is welcome — but never required.

Does it work on my phone?

Yes — any modern browser on iPhone, Android, tablet or computer.
