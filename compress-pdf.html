Shrink a PDF that's too big to send

The most common reason a PDF won't send is size — an email bounces, an upload form refuses it, a portal caps you at a few megabytes. Usually the culprit is images: scans and photos inside the document take up far more room than the text around them. Compressing those images is the quickest way to bring the file back to a sensible size.

This tool re-saves the images inside your PDF at a quality you choose, while leaving text and vector graphics completely untouched. The words stay crisp and selectable; only the heavy image data gets lighter. You see the before-and-after size, and there's no limit on how many PDFs you compress — all on your own device.

Why image-heavy PDFs shrink a lot — and text PDFs barely change

A PDF stores two very different kinds of content. Text and vector graphics are already compressed efficiently inside the file and lose almost no size when compressed again — there's simply little to remove. Embedded photos and scans are the opposite: they're large, and re-saving them at a lower resolution and quality can cut their size dramatically.

So the result depends entirely on what's inside. A PDF full of scanned pages or photos can shrink by 70% or more. A document that's mostly text won't change much — and that's normal. If your PDF barely shrinks, it usually means it was already efficient to begin with.

What stays perfect

Text stays sharp, selectable and searchable. Vector graphics like logos and line art keep their clean edges. Layout doesn't move — the document looks the same, just lighter. Only embedded photos are re-encoded, and only as much as you allow.

Is there a limit on how many PDFs I can compress?

No. Compress as many as you want, as often as you want — no cap, no account, no waiting between files.

Why did my text-only PDF barely shrink?

Because text takes up very little space to begin with, and it's already compressed inside the PDF. Compression here targets images, so a document without them has almost nothing to reduce — a sign your file was already lean.

Will the text still be selectable and searchable?

Yes. Only images are re-encoded. Text and vector elements are left exactly as they were, so you can still select, copy and search them.

Are my PDFs uploaded anywhere?

No. Compression runs entirely in your browser. Sensitive documents — contracts, medical forms, statements — stay on your device the whole time.

How much smaller will my PDF get?

It depends on the content. Image-heavy or scanned PDFs often drop by 70% or more; text-only PDFs change very little. The before-and-after figures show you the real result.

Can I compress several PDFs at once?

Yes. Drop in multiple files and each is compressed and downloaded separately. There's no limit.
